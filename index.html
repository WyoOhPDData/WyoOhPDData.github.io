NEW: 2019 Citation Demographics

It should surprise no one from Wyoming that the majority of people pulled over, cited, and fined are Black people that do not live in Wyoming. Mr. Ballinger attempts to justify this by pointing out the racial makeup of neighborhood areas, but his 52.2% number for Black drivers is wrong because he did not use a weighted average. 52.2% is what you get by averaging the percentages shown in the table, but it does not take into account the populations. Lincoln Heights is 87% Black, but only has about 3,000 residents. Springfield Township on the other hand has over 36,000. My weighted average predicts that 43% of drivers should be Black. Mr. Ballinger also fails to take into account Wyoming's 14% black population when calculating the average incorrectly. Ironically, including 14% in the incorrect calculation gives a result of 45% which is similar to the weighted average; this is coincidental.

These documents came from Wyoming mayor's court. They include a breakdown of citations given in the year 2019, and the monthly fees collected by the City. Most notably, the handwritten amount at the bottoms of each monthly reports labeled "To the city" goes to the Wyoming city general fund. The yearly total in 2019 for the offenses listed is $128,000 JUST to the city.
